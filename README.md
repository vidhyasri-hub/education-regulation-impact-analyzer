# education-regulation-impact-analyzer

Education policies, university regulations, accreditation guidelines, scholarship notifications, and circulars issued by bodies such as UGC, AICTE, NAAC, NIRF, and universities are usually written in complex academic, legal, and administrative language. Because of this, students, faculty, and institutions often struggle to understand the real meaning and impact of these regulations. People often do not know: 
● What the circular or regulation actually proposes or changes ● Why the regulation was introduced and what problem it aims to solve ● How the circular relates to earlier policies, amendments, or academic  frameworks 
● Which stakeholders, institutions, departments, or learners will be affected positively or negatively.
● What the short-term, medium-term, and long-term consequences of the regulation may be 
As a result, institutions may miss important compliance updates, students may remain unaware of opportunities or policy changes, and administrators may find it difficult to plan timely actions. 
This project aims to build an AI-powered platform that allows users to upload any education regulation document (PDF, HTML, or official link). The system will automatically analyze the document and convert it into a simple, easy-to-understand, stakeholder-friendly summary. It will explain the regulation’s purpose, historical background, affected stakeholders, institutional implications, opportunities, and risks across different timeframes. The platform will help people gain clear insights into education regulations without needing policy or compliance expertise.
